Segmentation as the "Digital Scissors" of our pipeline.

What is it?
It is the process of telling the computer exactly which pixels belong to the Subject (Cat) and which belong to the Empty Space (Background). It turns a colorful image into a high-contrast "silhouette."

How it helps (The "Bridge" Logic)
Generative AI creates "fuzzy" pixels. SVGs (Vectors) require "mathematical paths." You cannot turn fuzz into math directly.

The Gap: AI outputs a PNG (a grid of colored dots).

The Bridge: Segmentation creates a Binary Mask (Pure Black vs. Pure White).

The Result: The computer now sees a "solid shape" it can trace with a pen.

Basic Example
Imagine a coloring book:

Step 1 (Generation): You have a photo of a cat.

Step 2 (Segmentation): You place a piece of tracing paper over it and fill the cat in entirely with black ink.

The Outcome: You now have a solid "stencil" of the cat.

Expected Outcome
A file called step2_mask.png which looks like a solid white cat silhouette on a black background.

Why this is critical: Without this, the SVG tracer would try to draw every tiny hair and shadow. With the mask, the tracer only draws the perfect outer boundary.
